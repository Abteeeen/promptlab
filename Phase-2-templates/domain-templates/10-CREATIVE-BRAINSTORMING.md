## 🎯 TEMPLATE

```

Your specialty: Generating novel, feasible, impactful ideas under constraints.

BRAINSTORMING CHALLENGE:
Title: [WHAT WE'RE TRYING TO CREATE/SOLVE]

PROBLEM/OPPORTUNITY:
- Situation: [WHAT'S THE CONTEXT]
- Why it matters: [WHY SHOULD ANYONE CARE]
- Desired outcome: [WHAT WOULD SUCCESS LOOK LIKE]

TARGET CONTEXT:
- Who is this for: [AUDIENCE/CUSTOMER]
- What do they care about: [THEIR GOALS/CONSTRAINTS/PAIN POINTS]
- What's their limitation: [WHAT BLOCKS THEM NOW]

CONSTRAINTS (Creative boundaries):
- Budget: [FINANCIAL LIMITS]
- Time: [DEADLINE]
- Resources: [WHAT WE HAVE TO WORK WITH]
- Technical: [WHAT'S FEASIBLE]
- Brand: [BRAND GUIDELINES]
- Audience: [WHO WE CAN REACH]

INSPIRATION:
Show: [3-5 examples of ideas you like, even from other domains]
Avoid: [Things we should NOT copy]
Adjacent fields: [Fields we can steal ideas from]

BRAINSTORMING APPROACH:
- Divergence level: [WILD & CRAZY / PRACTICAL & NOVEL / INCREMENTAL INNOVATIONS]
- Quantity: [10-20 ideas / 30-50 ideas / 100+ ideas]
- Uniqueness: [Most unique ideas please / Balanced / Most feasible]
- Combinations allowed: [Mix and match ideas Y/N]

For EACH idea, show:
1. Core concept (what is it)
2. Why it works (what makes it clever)
3. How to execute (brief steps)
4. Why target audience cares (the hook)
5. Feasibility (how hard to do)
6. Differentiation (vs alternatives)

EVALUATION CRITERIA:
Rank ideas by:
[ ] Novelty (how different from the norm)
[ ] Feasibility (can we actually do this)
[ ] Impact (how much would it achieve the goal)
[ ] Appeal (would target audience care)
[ ] Scalability (can we expand it)

EXPECTED OUTPUT:
- [NUMBER] original ideas
- Ranked by viable/impact ratio
- Top 3 with detailed execution plans
- Synthesis idea (combining best elements)
```

## 🎨 REAL EXAMPLES

### Example 1: Product Concept Brainstorm
```
You are a product innovation expert.

CHALLENGE: Create a new fitness app that stands out in crowded market

CONTEXT:
- Problem: Fitness apps are everywhere, retention is terrible (50% drop in week 2)
- Opportunity: Make fitness social + fun + actually sticky
- Target: Millennials 25-35, busy professionals, want results not boring workouts
- Constraint: Lean team (3 people), $100k budget, 3-month launch

INSPIRATION:
- Duolingo (gamification mastery)
- Peloton (community + performance)
- TikTok (short, entertaining content)
- Twitch (live interaction)

BRAINSTORM [20 ideas, showing top 5]:

IDEA 1: "Fitness Battles" (Competitive micro-challenges)
- Concept: Users compete in 30-second fitness challenges (burpees, planks, etc)
- Hook: Live leaderboards, friend competition, community vs community
- Why clever: Removes boring solo workouts, adds competition + community
- Execute: Real-time video verification, auto-detect reps with AI, reward streaks
- Why target cares: Accountability + fun + social proof
- Feasibility: 3 months (build challenge framework, video detection, leaderboards)
- Risk/Mitigation: AI accuracy (start with human review, improve over time)

IDEA 2: "Fitness Streaks with Stories" (Narrative progression)
- Concept: Fitness app with story you unlock through workouts
- Hook: Each rep/run/workout = story progression in an engaging narrative
- Why clever: Turns fitness into storytelling (emotional engagement vs metrics brain)
- Execute: Write 100-page story, break into chapters, unlock as they hit workout goals
- Why target cares: Motivation beyond vanity (narrative pulling them forward)
- Feasibility: 2 months (story writing, content integration, basic app)
- Risk/Mitigation: Story must be engaging (hire good writer, test with beta)

IDEA 3: "Fitness Coaching via AI + Community" (Hybrid social)
- Concept: AI creates personalized workouts, community coaches provide live feedback
- Hook: "Your personal trainer is AI trained by top coaches, live humans in community"
- Why clever: Scalable coaching (AI) + human touch (community) = best of both
- Execute: AI generates weekly plan, users record workouts, community votes on form
- Why target cares: Results (personalized) + accountability (social)
- Feasibility: 8 weeks (AI model training, community features, video uploads)
- Risk/Mitigation: Community quality control (start small, vet members)

IDEA 4: "Fitness + Learning Adventure" (Gamified progression)
- Concept: Fitness app structured like RPG (levels, quests, unlocks)
- Hook: Each workout = XP, level up for better workouts, unlock new movement types
- Why clever: Hooks both fitness + gaming brains, progress feels visible
- Execute: Progression system (beginner → advanced), unlock new exercises, achievements
- Why target cares: Visible progress, gamification keeps engagement high
- Feasibility: 6 weeks (progression system, achievement framework)
- Risk/Mitigation: Progression must scale with fitness level (test extensively)

IDEA 5: "Social Fitness Livestream Platform" (Twitch for fitness)
- Concept: Platform where fitness creators go live, community joins, real-time interaction
- Hook: Feels like live group class (energy) but can join anyone anytime
- Why clever: Combines live energy with on-demand flexibility + community chat
- Execute: Streaming interface, live chat, recorded archives, creator payouts
- Why target cares: Group energy stimulates better workouts, social interaction
- Feasibility: 10 weeks (streaming infrastructure, creator tools, monetization)
- Risk/Mitigation: Creator quality (curate early creators, build brand)

---

TOP 3 TO PURSUE:

#1: "Fitness Battles" (Feasible + defensible)
- Highest potential (combines competition + community + short format)
- Technical feasible within timeline
- Creates habit loop (daily competition)

#2: "Fitness Streaks with Stories" (Fastest to launch)
- Can ship in 2 months
- Extremely unique (no competitor doing narrative fitness)
- Word-of-mouth potential

#3: "Fitness + Learning Adventure" (Best engagement)
- Bridges fitness + gaming (huge audience overlap)
- Proven model (Duolingo works this way)
- Scalable (expand game depth over time)

SYNTHESIS IDEA: "Fitness Adventures with Community Battles"
Core concept: Combine story progression (idea 2) + community competition (idea 1) + RPG elements (idea 4)
- Players progress through narrative (stay engaged emotionally)
- Unlock new challenges by hitting fitness milestones (motivation)
- Can compete with friends or globally (competition)
- Build community comment boards (social stickiness)

This hits: Motivation (story) + accountability (community) + fun (competition) + progress (RPG)
```

### Example 2: Marketing Campaign Brainstorm
```
You are a marketing creative director.

CHALLENGE: Launch new SaaS product with $50k budget

CONTEXT:
- Product: AI writing assistant for copywriters
- Target: Freelance copywriters, small agencies (DIY marketers)
- Problem: Hand-writing ad copy takes time, hard to test variations
- Goal: 500 signups in first month, establish thought leadership

CONSTRAINTS:
- Budget: $50k (paid ads: $30k, content: $15k, tools: $5k)
- Time: 4 weeks to launch
- Team: 1 marketer, 1 designer, 1 product person supporting

INSPIRATION:
- Notion's viral growth (templates, community)
- Buffer's "social proof" launch (transparent metrics)
- Figma's education program (creators as advocates)

BRAINSTORM [15 campaign ideas, showing strong candidates]:

CAMPAIGN 1: "Copy Challenge" (User-generated content + competition)
- Concept: 30-day challenge where copywriters create best ad copy using product
- Execution: 
  - Week 1: Launch challenge with daily prompts (email sequences, landing pages, ads)
  - Week 2-3: Users create copy with product, share results
  - Week 4: Announce winners (best copy, most creative, fastest creation)
- Prizes: $500 winners (publicly announced), feature in newsletter
- Why it works: Social proof (see product being used), free marketing (users sharing), engagement loop
- Budget: $3k (prizes) + $5k ad to promote challenge = $8k
- Expected: 200 challenge participants, 50% convert to users, 100 signups

CAMPAIGN 2: "Copy Templates Library" (Thought leadership + asset)
- Concept: Release 50 proven ad copy templates for free (massive value)
- Execution:
  - Create gated template library (landing page)
  - Each template: swipe-able, tested, includes metrics (open rate, CTR, conversion)
  - Promote heavily (social, ads, communities)
- Why it works: Lead magnet (convert free users), establishes expertise, solves real problem
- Budget: $5k (design/content) + $8k (promotion/ads)
- Expected: 1,000 emails captured, 15% become trial users = 150 signups

CAMPAIGN 3: "Copy Critique Video Series" (Educational content)
- Concept: Weekly 5-minute YouTube videos analyzing real ad copy (good/bad/improvements)
- Execution:
  - Find famous ads (Apple, Nike, DTC brands)
  - Break down what works, why it works, how to apply
  - End with: "Our product helped this creator improve X%"
- Why it works: Educational (viewers learn), builds channel, subtle product mentions
- Budget: $3k (video production/editing)
- Expected: 100 subscribers by month-end, 500 organic views, 30 signups

CAMPAIGN 4: "Copywriter Creator Program" (Influencer + affiliate)
- Concept: Recruit 10 top copywriters to create content promoting product
- Execution:
  - Find micro-influencers in copywriting space (5k-50k followers)
  - Give free access to product
  - Provide creative briefs (videos, testimonials, templates)
  - Pay commission on signups (10% / referral)
- Why it works: Authentic testimonials, reaches exact target audience, performance-based
- Budget: $8k (influencer fees) + 10% commission on signups
- Expected: 10 creators × 10-20 signups each = 100-200 signups

CAMPAIGN 5: "Copy Masterclass" (Live event + webinar)
- Concept: Free live 90-min masterclass "How to Write Ad Copy That Converts"
- Execution:
  - Promote aggressively (social, ads, email, communities)
  - Host live webinar (show product in action during teaching)
  - Record, repurpose (YouTube, clips)
  - Attendees get free trial
- Why it works: Authority + product demo + large audience capture
- Budget: $5k (social ads) + $2k (webinar platform/hosting)
- Expected: 500 attendees, 50% attend live, 30% start trial = 75 signups

---

TOP CAMPAIGNS TO EXECUTE (3-pronged approach):

PILLAR 1: "Copy Templates Library" (Lead generation)
- Fast to create (2 weeks)
- Proven model (lead magnets work)
- Builds content library
- Cost-effective ($13k, high ROI)

PILLAR 2: "Copywriter Creator Program" (Influencer marketing)
- Reaches exact audience
- Authentic testimonials
- Word-of-mouth multiplier
- Performance-based ($8k + commission)

PILLAR 3: "Copy Challenge" (Community + engagement)
- Creates viral loop (users sharing)
- Builds community early
- Great for social proof
- Medium cost ($8k)

TOTAL SPEND: ~$29k of $50k budget
REMAINING: $21k (paid ads boosting all pillar, buffer for optimization)

EXPECTED OUTCOMES:
- Templates library: 150 signups
- Creator program: 125 signups
- Copy challenge: 100 signups
- Paid ads boosting pillars: 75 signups
- Total: 450 signups (close to 500 goal)

TIMING:
- Week 1: Launch templates library (lead gen)
- Week 2: Announce creator program
- Week 3: Launch copy challenge
- Week 4: Run paid ads boosting all pillars + measure
```

## 🚀 BRAINSTORM FRAMEWORKS

### Divergence-Convergence
```
DIVERGENCE (Week 1): Generate 100+ wild ideas
- Quantity over quality
- Build on each other's ideas
- No criticism allowed

CONVERGENCE (Week 2): Evaluate, refine, combine
- Score ideas
- Merge best elements
- Create synthesis ideas
```

### SCAMPER Method
```
Substitute: Remove familiar element, add different one
Combine: Merge with something else
Adapt: Apply to different context
Modify: Change attributes (size, shape, color)
Put to another use: Different purpose
Eliminate: Remove core element
Reverse/Rearrange: Opposite of current
```

### Analogies from Other Fields
```
How does nature solve this?
How does gaming solve this?
How does social media solve this?
How does entertainment solve this?
```

## 💡 PRO TIPS

1. **Constraints = Creativity**
   - Unlimited options = paralyzes
   - Clear constraints = creative solutions
   - "We have $5k budget" > "Any budget"

2. **Build on Ideas**
   - "Yes, and..." not "No, but..."
   - Merge 2 good ideas into 1 great idea
   - Refine, don't reject

3. **Quantity First**
   - Generate 50 ideas before evaluating
   - Best ideas emerge from volume
   - Evaluation kills ideation

4. **Test Quick**
   - Validate assumptions with prototypes
   - Don't plan, test
   - Let users pick winners

5. **Borrow from Other Fields**
   - How do movies create engagement? (Narrative)
   - How does music create emotion? (Rhythm)
   - How does sports create loyalty? (Competition)
   - Apply to your domain
