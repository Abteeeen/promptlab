## 🎨 REAL EXAMPLES

### Example 1: LinkedIn Content Strategy
```
You are a LinkedIn growth specialist for B2B SaaS.

GOAL: Build thought leadership for [FOUNDER] + generate leads

AUDIENCE:
- Job titles: CTO, Head of Product, Engineering Manager
- Company size: 10-500 people
- Industry: Tech/SaaS
- Problem: [THEIR PAIN POINT]

CREATE: 4-week content calendar

CONTENT PILLARS (Weekly Mix):
- Week content: 30% (What we learned / What we noticed)
- Tips/frameworks: 40% (Actionable advice, how-tos)
- Behind-the-scenes: 20% (Culture, hiring, growth)
- Customer stories: 10% (Results achieved by customers)

POSTING PLAN:
- Frequency: 4x per week (Mon, Wed, Fri, Sunday)
- Times: 8am, 10am, 4pm, 7pm CET
- Post types: Mix of text posts (80%), thought leadership articles (20%)

ENGAGEMENT:
- Founder replies to comments personally
- Engage with 10 relevant accounts daily
- Quality over quantity (well-written beats frequent)

TONE: Expert but approachable, data-driven, practical

EXAMPLE WEEK 1:

POST 1 (Monday 8am):
Copy: "After analyzing 500 engineering teams, we found the #1 thing separating fast ones from slow ones isn't tools—it's how they communicate.

One thing: 3-hour Friday meetings.

Teams that cut these saw 40% faster shipping.

What's your biggest productivity killer?"

Visual: Infographic with meeting time vs shipping speed

Hashtags: #Engineering #Productivity #SoftwareDevelopment #Leadership

CTA: "Comment: What kills your team's productivity?"

---

POST 2 (Wednesday 10am):
Copy: "You don't need more tools. You need to do fewer things.

Here's the framework we use:

1. Monthly priorities (3-5 only)
2. Weekly focus (what unblocks others?)
3. Daily (show up, move needle, help your team)

Constraints are features."

Visual: Simple process diagram

Hashtags: #ProductManagement #Prioritization #Leadership

CTA: "How do you stay focused with infinite demands?"

---

POST 3 (Friday 4pm):
Copy: "Hiring story: We asked 'What was your biggest failure?' in interviews.

One candidate said: 'I've never failed.'

We passed.

Best hiring signal? People who know what they're bad at and what they've learned.

Do you ask this in interviews?"

Visual: None (text post)

Hashtags: #Hiring #Culture #Leadership

CTA: "Comment: What's your best interview question?"

---

POST 4 (Sunday 7pm):
Type: LinkedIn Article (1,000+ words)

Title: "How We Scaled Shipping Speed by 3x (Without Hiring More Engineers)"

Content:
- Problem we had
- What we tried (3 failed approaches)
- What finally worked
- Metrics (before/after)
- How to implement this
- What we learned

CTA: "Share this with your team / Thoughts?"
```

## 🚀 PLATFORM-SPECIFIC TIPS

### LinkedIn
- Long-form > short-form
- Thought leadership > self-promotion
- Data + insights = engagement
- Personal brand > company brand
- Comments matter more than likes

### Twitter
- Real-time is key
- Engagement = replies you get
- Threads perform well
- Memes/humor > polished
- Personality > professionalism

### Instagram
- Visual > copy
- Carousel > single image
- Reels > static posts
- Behind-scenes > polished
- Stories > feed (for engagement)

### TikTok
- Trends > originality
- Authenticity > polish
- Entertainment > education
- First 3 seconds critical
- Follow trends but add your twist

## 💡 PRO TIPS

1. **Show Personality**
   - Profiles are people, not companies
   - Share opinions, not neutral content
   - Be slightly controversial
   - Show your beliefs

2. **Engagement > Followers**
   - 10k followers with 0% engagement = useless
   - 1k followers with 20% engagement = goldmine
   - Engage first, grow later

3. **Platform > Content**
   - Same content doesn't work everywhere
   - Adapt to platform native format
   - LinkedIn: thoughtful, Twitter: quick, Instagram: visual
   - Know your platform

4. **Consistency > Perfection**
   - 5 good posts/week > 1 perfect post/week
   - Show up regularly
   - Algorithm rewards consistency

5. **Data > Guessing**
   - Track: Opens, clicks, comments, shares
   - See what works, do more
   - Test headlines, visuals, posting times
   - Let patterns emerge
